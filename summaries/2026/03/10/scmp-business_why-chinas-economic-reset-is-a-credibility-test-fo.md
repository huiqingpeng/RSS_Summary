【是什么 / What it is】
这篇文章分析中国2026年"两会"期间设定的经济增长目标下调，标志着北京正从追求增长速度转向追求增长质量，减少对房地产的依赖，更注重消费需求和生产力提升。香港作为离岸金融中心，其财政预算政策与北京的新方向高度一致，但能否成功承接这一转型所需的离岸融资和价格发现功能，仍是一个待验证的命题。

This article analyzes China's lowered 2026 economic growth target set during the "Two Sessions," signaling Beijing's shift from growth-at-pace to growth-with-durability—less reliance on property, more focus on consumption quality and productivity. Hong Kong's budget policy mirrors this direction, but whether the city can anchor the offshore financing and price discovery required by this reset remains an open question.

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】
这标志着中国经济模式的结构性转变：从供给侧产能扩张转向需求侧家庭消费提振，财政刺激更具针对性而非大水漫灌。对香港而言，这不仅是政策同步的问题，更是制度可信度的考验——在全球资本对中国经济重估的背景下，香港能否证明其作为离岸金融枢纽的独特价值，将决定其未来地位。

This marks a structural shift in China's economic model: from supply-side capacity building to demand-side household consumption boosting, with targeted rather than indiscriminate fiscal stimulus. For Hong Kong, this is not merely about policy alignment but institutional credibility—amid global capital's reassessment of China, whether Hong Kong can prove its unique value as an offshore financial hub will determine its future standing.
