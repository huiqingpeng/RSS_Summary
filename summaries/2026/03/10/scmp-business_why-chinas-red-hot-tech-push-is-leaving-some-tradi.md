【是什么 / What it is】

本文描述了中国地方政府正在推行"腾笼换鸟"的产业升级策略——通过腾退传统制造业用地，将资源和政策支持转向高科技和新兴产业企业，以迎合中央政府的创新优先政策。文章以宇树科技（Unitree）创始人王兴兴获得宁波市政府青睐为例，展现了这种政策倾斜如何重塑中国企业格局。

This article describes how Chinese local governments are implementing an industrial upgrade strategy colloquially known as "vacating the cage for new birds"—reallocating land from traditional manufacturers to hi-tech and emerging industries to align with Beijing's innovation-first policy priorities. Using Unitree founder Wang Xingxing's favored treatment by Ningbo government as an example, it illustrates how this policy tilt is reshaping China's corporate landscape.

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】

这种选择性资源分配正在加剧中国企业间的"温差"——科技新贵享受政策红利，而传统制造业则面临被边缘化的风险。这反映了中央政府经济优先级的快速转变，也揭示了地方政府在GDP竞争压力下可能出现的"一刀切"产业淘汰问题，可能对就业稳定和产业链完整性造成长期影响。

This selective resource allocation is intensifying a "temperature gap" among Chinese businesses—tech darlings enjoy policy dividends while traditional manufacturing faces marginalization. It reflects Beijing's rapidly shifting economic priorities and reveals how local governments, under GDP competition pressure, may indiscriminately phase out industries, potentially causing long-term impacts on employment stability and industrial chain integrity.
