【是什么 / What it is】

这篇文章报道了泰国警察系统中的一个致命漏洞：因将事故受害者误判为醉酒者而延误救治，导致本可挽救的生命死亡。文章以21岁女子Warissara的案例为核心——她在摩托车事故后因被闻到酒味而被送往警察局而非医院，最终因内出血在警局地板上死亡，同时提及另一名中风患者因同样误判而陷入昏迷的事件。

This article exposes a fatal flaw in Thailand's police system where accident victims are misidentified as drunk and denied timely medical care, resulting in preventable deaths. It centers on the case of 21-year-old Warissara, who died from internal bleeding on a police station floor after being sent there instead of a hospital due to alcohol smell detected at the scene, alongside another case of a stroke victim left in coma from the same assumption.

This pattern reveals how a "punish-first, treat-later" mindset in emergency systems creates systemic casualties, particularly prevalent in Southeast Asia where enforcement resources are strained.
