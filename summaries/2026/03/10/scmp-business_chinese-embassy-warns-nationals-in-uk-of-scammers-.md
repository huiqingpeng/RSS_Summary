【是什么 / What it is】
这是一则来自中国驻英国大使馆的安全警告，提醒在英中国公民警惕冒充香港廉政公署（ICAC）工作人员的电信诈骗。诈骗分子通过电话或网络联系受害者，谎称他们涉嫌洗钱等犯罪，要求配合"调查"并将资金转入所谓的"安全账户"。

This is a security warning from the Chinese Embassy in the United Kingdom, alerting Chinese citizens in Britain to phone scams involving fraudsters impersonating staff from Hong Kong's Independent Commission Against Corruption (ICAC). The scammers contact victims by phone or internet, falsely accusing them of crimes such as money laundering, and demand cooperation in a bogus "investigation" by transferring funds to so-called "safe accounts."

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】
此类诈骗专门针对海外华人社群，利用受害者对家乡执法机构的熟悉感和信任感，以及身处异国他乡的信息不对称和心理脆弱性。随着国际电信诈骗手段日益精密化，海外公民成为高风险目标，使馆预警机制对保护侨民财产安全至关重要。

This type of scam specifically targets overseas Chinese communities, exploiting victims' familiarity with and trust in home-country law enforcement agencies, as well as their information asymmetry and psychological vulnerability while living abroad. As international telecom fraud becomes increasingly sophisticated, overseas citizens have become high-risk targets, making embassy early-warning systems crucial for protecting diaspora property security.

【我能用什么 / How I can use it】
若在海外接到自称执法机构的电话，应立即挂断并通过官方渠道核实，切勿通过电话或网络转账至所谓"安全账户"。可将此案例分享给海外亲友以提高防范意识，同时关注驻外使领馆发布的安全提醒，建立"官方机构不会电话索财"的认知防线。

If receiving calls from self-proclaimed law enforcement agencies while overseas, immediately hang up and verify through official channels—never transfer money via phone or internet to so-called "safe accounts." Share this case with relatives and friends abroad to raise awareness, and regularly check security alerts from embassies and consulates, establishing a mental defense that "official agencies never demand money by phone."
