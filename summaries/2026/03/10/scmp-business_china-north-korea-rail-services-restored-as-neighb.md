【是什么 / What it is】
中朝铁路客运服务于2025年恢复，北京至平壤每周四班，丹东边境至平壤每日对开，其中两节车厢专供外国游客使用。这是自疫情中断以来，两国首次重启跨境客运列车，被视为朝鲜愿意修复与其最大贸易伙伴关系的试金石。

China-North Korea passenger rail services have resumed in 2025, with four weekly trains between Beijing and Pyongyang and daily services between the border city of Dandong and Pyongyang, including two carriages designated for foreign tourists. This marks the first restoration of cross-border passenger trains since the pandemic disruption and is viewed as a litmus test of Pyongyang's willingness to repair ties with its largest trading partner.

The move signals North Korea's gradual exit from prolonged isolation and may indicate its willingness to open up economically; China, as Pyongyang's largest trading partner, reinforces regional influence through transportation infrastructure rebuilding. The restoration of tourist access also provides a rare window to observe internal changes in North Korea, carrying dual geopolitical and economic significance.
