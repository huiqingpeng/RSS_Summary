【是什么 / What it is】

中国科技巨头正竞相推出基于OpenClaw（一款热门开源AI智能体软件）的低成本、易用型工具，包括腾讯的QClaw（可远程控制电脑）、MiniMax和智谱AI的集成方案、阿里云的低成本部署教程，以及字节跳动火山引擎的云端"开箱即用"版本ArkClaw，形成所谓的"龙虾热"（lobster fever）现象。

Chinese tech giants are racing to offer cheap, easy-to-use tools based on OpenClaw, a popular open-source AI agent software. These include Tencent's QClaw for remote PC control, integration solutions from MiniMax and Zhipu AI, Alibaba Cloud's low-cost deployment tutorials at 9.9 yuan, and ByteDance's cloud-based "out-of-the-box" ArkClaw—collectively forming a "lobster fever" phenomenon.

---

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】

这标志着AI智能体从实验室走向大众市场的关键转折点：巨头们通过降低技术门槛和成本（最低仅1.4美元），将复杂的AI Agent能力嵌入微信、钉钉等日常应用生态，可能加速企业自动化和个人生产力工具的普及，同时推动相关概念股（如MiniMax、智谱AI）大幅上涨。

This marks a critical inflection point for AI agents moving from labs to mass market. By lowering technical barriers and costs (as low as US$1.4), tech giants are embedding complex AI Agent capabilities into everyday ecosystems like WeChat and DingTalk, potentially accelerating enterprise automation and personal productivity tool adoption—while driving significant stock surges in related companies like MiniMax and Zhipu AI.

Individual users can try Tencent's QClaw for phone-to-PC remote control; developers can leverage Alibaba Cloud's 9.9-yuan tutorial for quick OpenClaw deployment and testing; enterprise users should note DingTalk's free API period (until March 31) for office automation integration; and those seeking zero-configuration can evaluate ByteDance's cloud-only ArkClaw, while monitoring plugin expansion in the open-source ecosystem.
