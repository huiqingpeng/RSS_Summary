【是什么 / What it is】
苹果公司在"3·15"国际消费者权益日前夕宣布，将其在中国App Store的标准佣金从30%降至25%，订阅续费及小型企业计划的佣金从15%降至12%。这是苹果在中国市场首次大幅下调应用商店抽成比例，也是其全球范围内罕见的佣金政策调整。
Apple announced a rare reduction in App Store commissions in China ahead of World Consumer Rights Day, lowering standard rates from 30% to 25% and subscription/small business rates from 15% to 12%. This marks Apple's first major commission cut in the Chinese market and is uncommon in its global policy history.

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】
此举反映出苹果面对中国监管压力和市场竞争的双重考量，尤其是央视"3·15"晚会前夕的敏感时机选择；同时获得腾讯、网易等头部游戏公司的公开支持，显示其有助于缓解与本土开发者的紧张关系，并可能重塑中国移动互联网生态的利益分配格局。
The move reflects Apple's response to dual pressures from Chinese regulators and market competition, particularly the timing before CCTV's annual consumer rights exposé; with public endorsement from Tencent and NetEase, it signals an effort to ease tensions with local developers and potentially reshape profit distribution in China's mobile internet ecosystem.

Developers can explore Apple's Small Business Program (12% rate for under $1M annual revenue) to optimize costs; businesses may evaluate shifting to subscription models to leverage lower renewal commissions; and stakeholders should monitor Chinese regulatory trends to anticipate whether Apple might extend similar adjustments to other markets.
