This article analyzes how the military actions taken by the Trump and Netanyahu administrations against Iran have escalated into a global economic crisis. It highlights that the Middle East conflict is generating worldwide ripple effects through energy markets, with a potential Iranian blockade of the Hormuz Strait creating the largest oil supply shock in history—three to four times greater than the 1973 and 1979 oil crises.

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】

这场冲突揭示了地缘政治决策与全球经济稳定之间的致命脱节。当油价逼近150美元/桶时，通胀压力、供应链中断和能源安全风险将波及每一个国家，无论其是否直接参与冲突。更重要的是，它暴露了"以武力遏制威胁"这一战略思维的反噬效应——美国长期试图避免的霍尔木兹海峡封锁，恰恰因对伊战争而成为现实。

This conflict reveals the fatal disconnect between geopolitical decisions and global economic stability. As oil prices approach $150 per barrel, inflationary pressures, supply chain disruptions, and energy security risks will cascade to every nation regardless of direct involvement. More critically, it exposes the blowback effect of "force-based threat containment" strategies—the very Hormuz Strait blockade the US long sought to avoid has materialized precisely because of the war against Iran.
