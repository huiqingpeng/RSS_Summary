In January 2025, Vietnam's trade surplus with the United States reached US$19 billion, surpassing both Mexico and China to become the world's largest surplus holder with the US. This phenomenon reflects the accelerating shift of global supply chains from China to Vietnam, highlighting Vietnam's pivotal role in the US-China trade rivalry.
