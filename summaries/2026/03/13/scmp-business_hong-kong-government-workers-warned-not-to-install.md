【是什么 / What it is】
香港数字政策办公室（DPO）近日警告所有政府部门不要在连接政府内部网络的设备上安装OpenClaw及其变体，理由是存在未授权数据访问、数据泄露和系统入侵等潜在安全风险。此前，中国内地多家券商、银行和政府机构已开始限制员工使用这款AI智能体。

The Hong Kong government's Digital Policy Office (DPO) has warned all government departments not to install OpenClaw or its variants on devices connected to internal networks, citing potential security risks including unauthorized data access, data leakage, and system intrusion. This follows reports that several mainland Chinese brokerages, banks, and government entities had begun restricting employee access to this AI agent.

This move reflects global governments' heightened vigilance over third-party AI tool security, particularly in scenarios involving sensitive government data. As AI agents become deeply integrated into workflows, "shadow IT" and supply chain security risks are emerging as new focal points for cybersecurity governance worldwide, signaling stricter AI application review regimes ahead.
