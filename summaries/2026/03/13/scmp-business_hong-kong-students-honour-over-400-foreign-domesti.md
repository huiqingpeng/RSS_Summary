【是什么 / What it is】
香港Renaissance College的学生每年举办"Jie Jie Day"（姐姐日）活动，向400多名外籍家庭佣工致敬，称她们为"第二位母亲"。该活动已持续14年，通过文化游戏、抽奖和社交机会，促进雇主与佣工之间的真诚联系。

Hong Kong's Renaissance College students host an annual "Jie Jie Day" event to honor over 400 foreign domestic helpers as "second mothers." Running for 14 years, the event uses cultural games, raffle draws, and networking opportunities to foster genuine connections between employers and helpers.

Foreign domestic helpers in Hong Kong often face class barriers and marginalization; this event breaks down the "artificial distance" between employers and workers while recognizing their contribution to multicultural families. It sets a replicable model of community engagement that prompts society to reconsider labor value and interpersonal respect.
