【是什么 / What it is】
这篇文章报道了霍尔木兹海峡过境船舶通过AIS信号伪造"中国船东/船员/货物"身份以规避伊朗封锁的现象。至少11艘悬挂巴拿马、马绍尔群岛、科威特和几内亚国旗的商船在周五下午更改了船舶自动识别系统（AIS）广播信息，宣称与中国有关联。

This article reports on a phenomenon where transiting vessels in the Strait of Hormuz are falsifying AIS signals to claim "Chinese owner/crew/cargo" identities to evade Iranian blockades. At least 11 commercial ships flying flags of Panama, the Marshall Islands, Kuwait, and Guinea altered their Automatic Identification System (AIS) broadcasts on Friday afternoon to declare Chinese affiliations.

This phenomenon reveals the vulnerability of global shipping amid geopolitical conflicts, and how "Chinese neutrality" has become a tradable strategic asset. As Middle East conflicts escalate, this "diplomatic bulletproofing" strategy is evolving into a systematic risk-mitigation mechanism, while also exposing a crisis of trust in international shipping identification systems.
