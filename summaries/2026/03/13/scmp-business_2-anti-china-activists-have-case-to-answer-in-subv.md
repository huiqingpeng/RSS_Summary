【是什么 / What it is】
香港高等法院裁定，两名前"香港支联会"领袖李卓人和邹幸彤须就煽动颠覆国家政权罪继续受审。法官认为控方证据显示，两人在2020年7月至2021年9月组织解散期间，持续宣扬结束"一党专政"的目标，可能构成煽动他人推翻中国共产党领导的犯罪行为。

Hong Kong's High Court has ruled that two former leaders of the Hong Kong Alliance in Support of Patriotic Democratic Movements of China, Lee Cheuk-yan and Chow Hang-tung, must stand trial for allegedly inciting subversion. The judges found that prosecution evidence suggested the activists, aged 69 and 41 respectively, committed the offence by persistently promoting an end to "one-party dictatorship" from July 2020 until the organization's dissolution in September 2021.

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】
此案是香港《国安法》实施后首宗涉及非暴力言论的颠覆国家政权案，标志着司法对"煽动"定义的扩展。法官虽驳回控方"中共领导不可变更"的宪法解释，但仍认定宣扬政治口号可构成犯罪，反映了香港言论自由与国家安全法律之间的张力持续收紧。

This case represents the first subversion trial under Hong Kong's National Security Law involving non-violent speech, signaling an expanded judicial interpretation of "incitement." While the judges rejected the prosecution's constitutional argument that CCP leadership is unchangeable, they still ruled that promoting political slogans could constitute a crime—reflecting the ongoing tightening balance between free expression and national security legislation in Hong Kong.
