【是什么 / What it is】

这篇文章报道了香港大埔太和邨王福苑（Wang Fuk Court）火灾后，近2000名住户对政府安置计划的不满。政府于3月2日派出100人的"联络团队"并提供查询热线，但两周后多数住户反映未被联系，且联络人员态度强硬、未能有效解答问题，甚至有建议长者拿现金到内地退休的不当言论。

This article reports on the dissatisfaction of nearly 2,000 residents of Wang Fuk Court in Tai Po, Hong Kong, following a fire incident. Despite the government deploying a 100-strong engagement team and an inquiry hotline on March 2, most residents reported no contact after two weeks. Those who were contacted found officers pushy and unhelpful, with some even suggesting elderly residents take cash and retire in mainland China.

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】

这起事件揭示了大型公共危机中政府与民众沟通机制的失效——联络团队规模庞大却效率低下，反映出"重形式轻实效"的官僚问题。同时，"赴内地退休"的建议也触及香港社会敏感的跨境养老议题，以及政府安置政策是否真正尊重居民选择权的争议。

This incident exposes the failure of government-resident communication mechanisms during large-scale public crises—a massive outreach team with poor effectiveness reflects bureaucratic formalism over substance. The suggestion to "retire in mainland China" also touches on sensitive cross-border elderly care issues in Hong Kong society, raising questions about whether resettlement policies truly respect residents' autonomy.

在组织危机应对或社区沟通项目时，应优先建立小规模但训练有素的专职团队，而非追求人数规模；同时需设计明确的FAQ和决策流程，确保一线人员能即时回应核心关切。对于涉及重大人生选择的政策沟通，应避免替对方做决定，而是提供清晰、可比的信息选项。

When organizing crisis response or community engagement projects, prioritize small, well-trained dedicated teams over headcount; design clear FAQs and decision workflows to ensure frontline staff can address core concerns immediately. For policies involving major life decisions, avoid making choices for stakeholders—instead provide clear, comparable information options.
