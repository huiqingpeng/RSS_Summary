【是什么 / What it is】
新加坡警方发出警告，指出越来越多马来西亚诈骗分子针对新加坡受害者实施"政府官员冒充诈骗"，诱骗受害者将现金或黄金交给在新加坡活动的"钱骡"（mules），以规避线上转账的安全管控。这种跨境转移模式是诈骗团伙应对银行加强反诈措施后的新转向。

The Singapore police have warned that an increasing number of Malaysian scammers are targeting Singapore-based victims through "government official impersonation scams," pressuring them to hand over cash or gold to "mules" operating in Singapore to bypass stricter online transfer safeguards. This cross-border collection method represents a pivot by scam syndicates in response to enhanced banking security measures.

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】
这一趋势显示诈骗手段正在快速进化——当线上转账渠道被收紧时，犯罪分子立即转向物理资产转移和跨境协作，利用两国执法边界制造追赃难度。超过50名马来西亚人自2024年5月以来因此被起诉，说明此类犯罪网络已形成规模化运作，对区域金融安全和公众信任构成持续威胁。

This trend demonstrates the rapid evolution of scam tactics—when online transfer channels are tightened, criminals immediately pivot to physical asset transfers and cross-border collaboration, exploiting jurisdictional boundaries to hinder asset recovery. With over 50 Malaysians prosecuted since May 2024, these criminal networks have clearly scaled up operations, posing persistent threats to regional financial security and public trust.

Individuals should remain vigilant against any calls demanding "in-person delivery of cash/valuables to prove fund legitimacy"—legitimate enforcement agencies never make such requests. Cross-border law enforcement agencies need tighter real-time intelligence sharing mechanisms and joint strategies targeting this hybrid "online scam + offline collection" approach.
