【是什么 / What it is】
这篇文章分析了缅甸军政府在2025年举行的大选及其结果。尽管军方支持的联邦巩固与发展党（USDP）在选举中赢得超过80%的议会席位，但文章指出这并未赋予军政府真正的合法性，因为其实际得票率仅为44%，且权力结构仍受制于军方高层与USDP领导人之间的微妙谈判。

This article analyzes the 2025 election held by Myanmar's military junta and its outcomes. Despite the military-backed Union Solidarity and Development Party (USDP) winning over 80% of parliamentary seats, the article argues this did not confer genuine legitimacy on the junta, as its actual vote share was only 44%, and the power structure remains subject to delicate negotiations between military top brass and USDP leaders.

This "staged election" reveals the core dilemma facing authoritarian regimes: formal electoral victories cannot translate into substantive governing legitimacy. The article identifies two critical vulnerabilities—internal power tensions within the ruling clique and the massive gap between vote share and seat allocation—which may foreshadow limitations in the junta's long-term control capacity, offering important insights for understanding authoritarian system stability.
