【是什么 / What it is】
泰国国王于周六正式主持新一届国会开幕，但2月8日的大选结果正面临宪法法院审查——监察专员办公室收到21起公众投诉，称选票上的条形码和二维码可能违反法律，破坏投票保密性。监察专员已向宪法法院提交请愿，要求审议此案。

Thailand's King Maha Vajiralongkorn formally opened the new parliament on Saturday, but the February 8 general election result faces Constitutional Court scrutiny after the Office of the Ombudsman received 21 public complaints alleging that bar codes and QR codes on ballots may violate the law and undermine voting secrecy. The ombudsman has petitioned the court to consider the case.

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】
此案触及民主选举的核心原则——选票保密性，泰国宪法法院曾在2006年以投票未保密为由废除过一次选举，历史 precedent 使此次审查结果具有重大政治风险。若选举被推翻，可能影响Bhumjaith党（赢得至少191席）与Pheu Thai党组建稳定执政联盟的计划，延续泰国长期的政治动荡。

The case touches on the core principle of democratic elections—ballot secrecy—and the Constitutional Court previously annulled a 2006 election on grounds of non-secret voting, making this review politically high-risk. If overturned, it could derail plans by the victorious Bhumjaithai party (with at least 191 seats) to form a stable ruling coalition with Pheu Thai party, potentially extending Thailand's prolonged political volatility.
