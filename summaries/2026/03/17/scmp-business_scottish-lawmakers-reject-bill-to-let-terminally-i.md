Scottish Parliament lawmakers rejected a bill that would have allowed terminally ill adults to seek assistance in ending their lives, with a vote of 69 against and 57 in favor. This represents the latest legislative attempt at legalizing "assisted dying" in the UK, where a similar bill for England and Wales is currently stalled in the British Parliament.
