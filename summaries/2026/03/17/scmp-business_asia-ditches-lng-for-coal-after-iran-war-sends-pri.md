【是什么 / What it is】
本文报道了亚洲多国在美国-以色列对伊朗战争导致液化天然气（LNG）供应中断和价格飙升的背景下，正从天然气转向煤炭发电的现象。由于霍尔木兹海峡航运几乎停滞、卡塔尔暂停出口，亚洲现货LNG价格已翻倍至三年高点，迫使孟加拉国、巴基斯坦、菲律宾、越南和泰国等国增加煤电以保障能源供应和降低成本。

This article reports that Asian countries are switching from natural gas to coal-fired power generation amid supply disruptions and soaring LNG prices caused by the US-Israeli war on Iran. With shipping through the Strait of Hormuz nearly halted and Qatar suspending exports, Asian spot LNG prices have doubled to three-year highs, forcing Bangladesh, Pakistan, the Philippines, Vietnam, and Thailand to increase coal power to ensure energy supply and reduce costs.

This phenomenon reveals how geopolitical conflicts instantly impact global energy markets and the reality that energy security takes precedence over energy transition. As the world's largest LNG demand market, Asia's return to coal may undermine global emission reduction efforts, while also demonstrating the risks of single-energy dependence—marking the second major supply crisis in four years since the 2022 Russia-Ukraine conflict.
