This article explores how temperature significantly affects flavor perception in filter coffee, and how specialty cafés are integrating "temperature-guided tasting" into customer service. It combines competition practices from the World Brewers Cup (WBrC), scientific research data, and industry expert insights to illustrate how coffee flavors evolve as it cools from hot to room temperature.

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】
随着零售咖啡价格上涨，消费者对精品咖啡的期待值提升，温度管理正成为高端咖啡馆差异化竞争的新维度。研究表明，甜度在44°C左右达到峰值，而苦味会随温度降低而减弱，这一科学认知正在改变传统的"咖啡越热越好"的消费观念。

As retail coffee prices rise, consumer expectations for specialty coffee increase, making temperature management a new dimension for differentiation among high-end cafés. Research shows sweetness peaks around 44°C while bitterness fades as temperature drops—scientific insights that are challenging the traditional "hotter is better" coffee consumption mindset.

Coffee professionals can preheat vessels to stabilize temperature, use tools like the Nucleus Compass thermometer, and prepare simple tasting guide cards for customers; encourage patrons to taste upon arrival, then experience flavor evolution as it cools. For experience-focused venues, consider adopting an "omakase"-style approach with structured multi-temperature tasting flights.
