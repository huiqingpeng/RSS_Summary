【是什么 / What it is】

This article analyzes political and military crises that erupted in Iran, Panama, Venezuela and Greenland over the past 12 months, arguing that while they appear as separate events on the surface, they collectively point to a geopolitical contest over critical global shipping routes, ports, oil resources and strategic chokepoints. The author contends these crises are not coincidental but reflect how strategic locations on the map have become focal points of great power competition.

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】
这些危机正在重塑全球能源和贸易格局：中国在委内瑞拉和伊朗两个重要石油市场的活动空间被压缩，亚洲整体面临运费上涨和保险成本增加的双重冲击，而俄罗斯则获得了更多向中国出售原油的机会。这揭示了"路线紧缩"时代亚洲作为制造业基地和海湾石油目的地所承受的系统性风险——贸易冲击与能源冲击在此交汇。

These crises are reshaping global energy and trade patterns: China is losing maneuvering room in two key oil markets (Venezuela and Iran), Asia as a whole faces dual shocks from rising freight costs and insurance premiums, while Russia gains more opportunities to sell crude to China. This reveals the systemic risks Asia bears as both a manufacturing hub and destination for Gulf oil in an era of "route tightening"—where trade shocks and energy shocks converge.
