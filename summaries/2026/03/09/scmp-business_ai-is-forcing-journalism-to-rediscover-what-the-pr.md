This article examines how AI is disrupting journalism, arguing that the profession's core value lies not in "writing" as an output form, but in "verification of truth" as a professional function. Using the architect analogy, the author redefines journalists' competitive advantage: not producing text, but validating facts.

This perspective carries industry-transforming significance: it liberates journalism from "content production" anxiety toward its essential role of "trust-building."
