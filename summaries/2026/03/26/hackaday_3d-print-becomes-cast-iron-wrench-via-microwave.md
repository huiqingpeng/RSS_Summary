【是什么 / What it is】
这篇文章介绍了一种将消费级3D打印与微波熔炼相结合，用铸铁铸造金属零件的DIY工艺。该方法利用碳化硅坩埚吸收微波能量产生高温，将金属熔化后浇铸到由3D打印塑料件制成的砂型模具中，最终获得实心金属零件。
This article introduces a DIY process combining consumer-grade 3D printing with microwave melting to cast metal parts from cast iron. The method uses silicon carbide crucibles that absorb microwave energy to generate high temperatures, melting metal that is then poured into sand molds created from 3D-printed plastic parts to produce solid metal components.

This technique breaks through the limitation of consumer-grade 3D printing being restricted to soft plastics, allowing hobbyists to achieve near-industrial casting strength at minimal cost. It represents a significant advancement in distributed manufacturing and the maker movement, bringing metalworking from factories to personal workshops, though it still has limitations in precision and strength.
