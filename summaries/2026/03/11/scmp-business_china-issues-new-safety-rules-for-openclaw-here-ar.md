【是什么 / What it is】
中国工信部下属的国家漏洞数据库（NVDB）发布了针对OpenClaw（一款以"龙虾"为吉祥物、正在快速普及的AI智能体平台）的首份安全使用指南。该指南与AI智能体提供商、漏洞平台运营商及网络安全公司合作制定，列出了六项"应该做"和六项"不应该做"的安全实践，以应对当前AI智能体应用热潮中的安全风险。

China's National Vulnerability DataBase (NVDB), under the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT), has issued its first safety guidelines for OpenClaw—an AI agent platform featuring a lobster mascot that is experiencing rapid adoption. Developed in collaboration with AI agent providers, vulnerability platform operators and cybersecurity firms, the advisory outlines six "dos" and six "don'ts" to address security risks amid the current frenzy of AI agent deployment.

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】
这标志着监管机构首次将AI智能体纳入正式的网络安全治理框架，反映了AI技术从实验性工具向关键基础设施转变的趋势。随着OpenClaw等AI智能体被广泛用于自动化任务处理，其暴露的攻击面（如第三方技能市场、浏览器劫持、权限过度授予）已成为国家级安全关切，预示着全球范围内AI安全合规监管将加速收紧。

This marks the first time regulators have formally incorporated AI agents into cybersecurity governance frameworks, reflecting the shift of AI technology from experimental tools to critical infrastructure. As platforms like OpenClaw are increasingly deployed for automated task processing, their exposed attack surfaces—such as third-party skill markets, browser hijacking, and excessive permission grants—have become national security concerns, signaling accelerated tightening of AI safety compliance regulations globally.
