【是什么 / What it is】
香港《基本住房单位条例》于3月1日正式生效，要求分间楼宇单位（俗称"劏房"）必须符合最低标准（至少86平方英尺、2.3米楼高、有窗户和厕所等）并经专业人士认证方可合法出租。房东可选择申请认证或登记一年宽限期，后者允许其在2030年2月前完成整改期间继续合法出租。
The Basic Housing Units Ordinance took effect on Hong Kong on March 1, mandating that subdivided flats must meet minimum standards (at least 86 sq ft, 2.3m ceiling height, window and toilet, etc.) and obtain professional certification to be legally leased. Landlords can either seek certification or register for a one-year grace period, allowing legal leasing until February 2030 while completing required rectifications.

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】
数据显示政策推行两周后仅1名房东申请认证，却有316宗申请涉及约1,160个单位登记宽限期，反映房东普遍选择拖延而非立即合规，可能源于改造成本高昂或对未来执法力度存疑。违规房东最高面临30万港元罚款及3年监禁，但宽限期机制可能削弱政策即时改善居住环境的效力。
Statistics reveal only one landlord sought certification after two weeks, while 316 applications covering ~1,160 flats registered for the grace period, indicating landlords generally prefer delay over immediate compliance—likely due to high renovation costs or skepticism about enforcement. Non-compliant landlords face up to HK$300,000 fines and 3 years' imprisonment, yet the grace period mechanism may dilute the policy's immediate impact on living conditions.
