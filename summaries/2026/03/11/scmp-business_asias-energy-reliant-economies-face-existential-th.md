This article discusses the potential risks facing Asia's energy-import-dependent economies—if the Iran war lasts for several weeks, it could cause prolonged energy market disruptions, driving up fuel costs and widening trade deficits. The deterioration in energy terms of trade will directly hit Asian emerging economies, with Morocco, Pakistan, and Thailand already seeing energy deficits exceed 4% of GDP, while China, the Philippines, and Indonesia are also affected.
