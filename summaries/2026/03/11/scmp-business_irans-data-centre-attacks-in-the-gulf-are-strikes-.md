【是什么 / What it is】

This article discusses recent Iranian drone attacks on data centers in the Gulf region, marking a shift in geopolitical risk from traditional oil infrastructure to digital infrastructure. It highlights that this transition reflects how the Gulf states' economic diversification strategies—particularly in AI and cloud computing—have become new targets for attacks.

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】

数据中心袭击直接威胁海湾国家"后石油时代"的经济转型，尤其是其人工智能雄心所依赖的廉价、充足且可靠的电力优势。这一演变迫使企业和投资者重新评估风险模型，因为传统的危机应对手册（针对石油设施设计）已不再适用于保护数字基础设施。

Data center attacks directly threaten the Gulf states' post-oil economic transformation, especially the competitive advantage of cheap, abundant and reliable power that underpins their AI ambitions. This evolution forces businesses and investors to reassess risk models, as the conventional crisis playbook—designed for oil facilities—no longer applies to protecting digital infrastructure.
