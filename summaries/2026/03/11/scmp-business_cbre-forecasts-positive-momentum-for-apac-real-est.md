【是什么 / What it is】

本文是世邦魏理仕（CBRE）发布的2026年亚太区商业地产市场预测报告，指出随着投资者信心回升、融资环境改善及未来供应减少，该区域商业地产投资与租赁活动有望双双回暖，办公楼更自2020年以来首次重返最受青睐的资产类别。

This article presents CBRE's forecast for the Asia Pacific commercial real estate market in 2026, highlighting a projected rebound in both investment and leasing activity driven by improving investor sentiment, easing financing conditions, and reduced future supply—with offices returning as the most preferred asset class for the first time since 2020.

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】

该预测标志着亚太商业地产市场从疫情后低迷中实质性复苏，东京连续七年稳居跨境投资首选、香港重返前五，反映区域资本流动格局正在重塑；同时办公楼空置率收紧、物流租金增长放缓、酒店资产转型等结构性变化，为投资者和租户提供了重新配置资产的窗口期。

This forecast signals a substantive recovery of APAC commercial real estate from post-pandemic sluggishness, with Tokyo's seven-year reign as top cross-border destination and Hong Kong's return to the top five reflecting a reshaping of regional capital flows; meanwhile, structural shifts—tightening office vacancy, moderating logistics rental growth, and hotel asset conversions—create a window for portfolio reconfiguration by investors and occupiers.

Investors may prioritize prime offices and data centers in Tokyo, Sydney, Singapore, Seoul, and Hong Kong, while evaluating asset repurposing opportunities such as hotel-to-student-housing conversions; corporate occupiers should leverage the supply peak to secure quality office space in core CBDs or consolidate into automated logistics facilities; retailers need to act swiftly to secure prime locations for experiential stores amid constrained supply.
