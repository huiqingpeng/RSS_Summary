【是什么 / What it is】
这篇文章分析了中东地缘政治冲突（特别是伊朗对卡塔尔天然气设施的袭击）如何通过化肥供应链影响亚洲粮食安全。卡塔尔等海湾国家生产的液化天然气是制造氨和尿素的关键原料，而这些化肥是亚洲水稻等主粮作物种植的基础投入品。

This article analyzes how Middle East geopolitical conflicts—specifically Iran's attack on Qatar's natural gas facility—threaten Asian food security through the fertilizer supply chain. Liquefied natural gas from Gulf states like Qatar is a critical feedstock for producing ammonia and urea, which are essential inputs for growing staple crops such as rice across Asia.

The Gulf region accounts for 45% of global urea exports, so any supply disruption quickly drives up fertilizer prices and subsequently food prices. With urea prices already surging over 40% to $700/tonne, continued conflict could lead to empty shelves and record food prices by autumn, severely impacting Asia's food-importing nations.
