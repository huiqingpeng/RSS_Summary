【是什么 / What it is】

这篇文章介绍了一种修复CRT显示器"白内障"（屏幕安全玻璃与显像管之间脱胶分层）的创新方法：通过注射UV固化树脂来重新粘合，而非传统的加热拆除玻璃。作者以一台1990年代的20英寸Macintosh Trinitron显示器为例，展示了这一修复过程。

This article introduces an innovative method for repairing CRT "cataracts" (delamination between the safety glass and the tube): injecting UV-cure resin to re-bond the layers, rather than the traditional heat-based glass removal. The author demonstrates this repair on a 1990s-era 20" Macintosh Trinitron display.

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】

CRT显示器在复古计算和游戏社区中仍有重要价值，但"白内障"问题是常见的老化故障，传统修复方法存在损坏风险。这种树脂注射技术更安全、可逆（失败仍可改用热空气法），且保留了原装安全玻璃，为 vintage 硬件维护提供了实用的新选择。

CRT displays remain valuable in retro computing and gaming communities, but "cataracts" are a common aging failure, and traditional repairs risk damage. This resin injection technique is safer, reversible (can still switch to heat if it fails), and preserves the original safety glass—offering a practical new option for vintage hardware preservation.

If you have a CRT with early-stage cataract delamination, try using a syringe and UV resin to fill the gap around the edges (reference: ~1ml resin per cm²). This works best when delamination hasn't extended far beyond the edges; document your process and keep the heat-gun method as a backup to balance innovation with risk management.
