【是什么 / What it is】
中国"两会"释放的政策信号表明，中美科技竞争正进入一个以技术自主为核心的新时代。文章分析了2024年政府工作报告中的科技投入增长目标（研发经费增长10%、基础研究增长超16%），以及中国在半导体、人工智能、量子计算等战略领域的布局，揭示两国关系将呈现"激烈竞争+有限合作"的新格局。

China's "two sessions" policy signals indicate that China-US tech competition is entering a new era centered on technological self-reliance. The article analyzes the increased R&D funding targets in the 2024 government work report (10% for R&D, over 16% for basic research) and China's strategic positioning in semiconductors, AI, quantum computing and other fields, revealing a new paradigm of "intensified competition with limited cooperation."

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】
这标志着全球科技格局从"相互依赖"向"平行体系"加速演变，技术脱钩风险持续上升。对中国而言，外部制裁压力倒逼自主创新投入；对美国而言，出口管制工具的效果与反噬效应并存。这一趋势将重塑全球供应链、人才流动和科研合作模式，对跨国企业和投资者的战略决策产生深远影响。

This signals an accelerating shift in the global tech landscape from "interdependence" toward "parallel systems," with rising decoupling risks. For China, external sanctions are driving forced investment in indigenous innovation; for the US, export controls carry both intended effects and unintended blowback. This trend will reshape global supply chains, talent flows and research collaboration models, profoundly impacting strategic decisions for multinational corporations and investors.

Tech professionals should monitor investment and career opportunities in domestic substitution supply chains while tracking international technical standards; business decision-makers need to assess supply chain resilience and formulate "China+1" or "US+1" diversification strategies; researchers can explore cooperation windows in non-sensitive areas such as climate change and public health, maintaining academic networks through the cracks of competition.
