【是什么 / What it is】
香港城市大学的一项研究发现，搁浅死亡的中华白海豚和印太江豚体内检测出液晶单体（LCMs）——一种用于制造笔记本电脑、电视和智能手机LCD屏幕的有毒化学物质。约88%的样本中发现了可检测浓度的LCMs，且该污染物能够突破保护大脑的血脑屏障。

A study by City University of Hong Kong found toxic liquid crystal monomers (LCMs)—chemicals used to manufacture LCD screens for laptops, TVs and smartphones—in stranded and deceased Chinese white dolphins and Indo-Pacific finless porpoises. About 88% of samples contained detectable concentrations of LCMs, with the pollutants capable of breaching the blood-brain barrier that protects the brain.

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】
这是首次证实电子废弃物中的LCMs能够穿透海豚的血脑屏障，而该屏障同样存在于人类和其他动物体内，暗示了潜在的跨物种健康风险。研究揭示了电子消费产品的生命周期末端污染问题，对濒危海洋哺乳动物保护及人类环境健康都具有警示意义。

This is the first confirmation that LCMs from e-waste can penetrate the blood-brain barrier in dolphins—a barrier also present in humans and other animals—suggesting potential cross-species health risks. The research highlights end-of-life pollution from consumer electronics, with implications for both endangered marine mammal conservation and human environmental health.

【我能用什么 / How I can use it】
个人可延长电子产品使用周期、选择正规回收渠道，减少电子垃圾产生；企业和政策制定者应推动更严格的电子废弃物处理法规与绿色设计标准。该研究也为环境科学、公共卫生领域的进一步研究提供了方向，可延伸至人类神经毒性风险评估。

Individuals can extend product lifecycles and use certified recycling channels to reduce e-waste; businesses and policymakers should push for stricter e-waste regulations and green design standards. The study also opens avenues for further research in environmental science and public health, potentially extending to human neurotoxicity risk assessment.
