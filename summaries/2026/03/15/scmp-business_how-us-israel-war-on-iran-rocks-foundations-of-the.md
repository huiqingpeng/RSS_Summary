【是什么 / What it is】

本文分析了2020年《亚伯拉罕协议》（Abraham Accords）的地缘政治基础正受到美以联合对抗伊朗的最新冲突冲击。该协议曾促成以色列与阿联酋、巴林等阿拉伯国家关系正常化，绕过巴勒斯坦问题建立反伊朗安全同盟，但如今这一"以安全换合作"的逻辑因地区局势升级而面临严峻考验。特朗普第二任期正试图将沙特、叙利亚等国纳入该框架，以强化情报共享、导弹防御协调和经济一体化。

This article analyzes how the geopolitical foundation of the 2020 Abraham Accords is being shaken by the latest US-Israel confrontation against Iran. The accords previously enabled normalization between Israel and Arab states like the UAE and Bahrain, bypassing the Palestinian issue to build an anti-Iran security alliance, but this "security-for-cooperation" logic now faces severe strain due to regional escalation. In his second term, Trump is attempting to expand the framework to include Saudi Arabia, Syria and others to strengthen intelligence sharing, missile defense coordination, and economic integration.

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】

这一动态揭示了中东安全架构的深层悖论：阿拉伯国家为应对伊朗威胁而结盟，却可能因盟友的激进行动而陷入更大风险。协议扩张若成功，将重塑中东权力格局，使美国主导的安全体系进一步固化；若失败，则可能暴露"跳过巴勒斯坦问题"式外交的局限性，迫使各方重新考虑地区稳定的根本前提。对中国、欧盟等外部行为体而言，这关系到能源安全、贸易路线及多边外交空间的重新配置。

This dynamic reveals a deep paradox in Middle East security architecture: Arab states allied against Iran may face greater risks precisely due to their allies' aggressive actions. Successful expansion of the accords would reshape regional power dynamics and further entrench a US-led security system; failure, however, could expose the limitations of "Palestine-skipping" diplomacy and force a reconsideration of fundamental prerequisites for regional stability. For external actors like China and the EU, this concerns the reconfiguration of energy security, trade routes, and multilateral diplomatic space.
