This article cites a US think tank study based on open-source intelligence, indicating that most of China's nuclear warheads are stored at a single facility—Base 67 (also known as Hongchuan)—deep within the Qinling Mountains, accessible only via one narrow road. The report argues this highly centralized storage creates significant strategic vulnerability.
