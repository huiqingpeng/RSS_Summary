This article discusses the indirect cyber risks facing Southeast Asian businesses—as conflict escalates in the Middle East (particularly involving Iran), state-sponsored cyberattacks targeting that region can spill over to geographically distant Southeast Asian companies through globally interconnected supply chains, cloud services, and data networks. It cites the CEO of Malaysian cybersecurity firm AKATI Sekurity emphasizing that modern cyber threats are no longer constrained by geographic boundaries.

This trend exposes systemic vulnerabilities in globalized digital infrastructure: the UAE faces 90,000–200,000 intrusion attempts daily with over 70% from state-sponsored or APT groups, while Southeast Asian companies often remain underprotected due to "geographic thinking" blind spots. It signals that cybersecurity must shift from localized defense to global risk awareness, with cyber "blast radius" effects from geopolitical conflicts becoming a normalized challenge for multinational enterprises.
