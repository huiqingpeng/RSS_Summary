【是什么 / What it is】
香港出口信用保险局（HKECIC）在地缘政治风险上升的背景下，采取与常规贸易信用保险模式相反的策略——维持低保费并提供优惠措施，以支持本地出口商（特别是中小企业）应对不确定性。该机构认为香港对中东地区的直接贸易敞口较小（仅占整体贸易约2.5%），因此风险可控。

The Hong Kong Export Credit Insurance Corporation (HKECIC) is adopting a counter-cyclical approach contrary to conventional trade credit insurance models—maintaining low premiums and offering concessions to support local exporters, especially SMEs, amid rising geopolitical risks. The agency considers risks manageable given Hong Kong's limited direct trade exposure to the Middle East (only about 2.5% of total trade).

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】
这一政策体现了政府支持机构在经济不确定性时期主动承担风险、稳定商业信心的角色，而非将风险完全转嫁给企业。同时，免费装运前风险保障等针对性措施直接回应了中小企业在订单取消场景下的脆弱性，有助于维护香港作为国际贸易枢纽的竞争力。

This policy demonstrates how government-backed institutions can proactively absorb risks and stabilize business confidence during economic uncertainty, rather than fully transferring risks to enterprises. Targeted measures like free pre-shipment risk cover directly address SMEs' vulnerability to order cancellations, helping maintain Hong Kong's competitiveness as an international trade hub.

Exporters with annual sales below HK$50 million should proactively apply for HKECIC's free pre-shipment risk cover to mitigate losses from buyer order cancellations. Exporters in other regions can reference this model to evaluate whether local export credit agencies offer similar concessions or advocate for policy support. Businesses should also regularly review their trade geographic distribution to identify and diversify concentrated regional risks.
