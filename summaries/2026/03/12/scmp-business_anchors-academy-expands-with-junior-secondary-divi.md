【是什么 / What it is】

Anchors Academy, a leading Hong Kong private school for kindergarten and primary education, is expanding to include Junior Secondary (Years 7-9) and partnering with UK's historic St Bees School (founded 1583) to establish St Bees (Anchors) Sixth Form College in Hong Kong, creating a complete K-12 educational pathway for students aged 2-18.

香港私立名校Anchors Academy宣布扩展初中部（7至9年级），并与英国历史悠久的圣比斯学校（1583年创校）合作，在香港成立圣比斯（Anchors）预科书院，打造覆盖2至18岁学生的完整K-12教育体系。

---

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】

This development addresses growing demand for seamless "through-train" international education in Hong Kong, eliminating the disruption of school transitions. The partnership combines Anchors' STEAM-focused innovation with St Bees' 440-year academic heritage, while offering students direct exposure to Cambridge and Oxford professors—positioning Hong Kong as a hub for premium British-style international education in Asia.

此举回应了香港家长对无缝"一条龙"国际教育的旺盛需求，避免升学转校的断层。合作融合了Anchors的STEAM创新教育与圣比斯440年的学术传统，更让学生直接接触剑桥、牛津教授授课，巩固香港作为亚洲英式国际教育枢纽的地位。

【我能用什么 / How I can use it】

Parents with children currently in Anchors' primary program or aged 10-14 should register interest now for 2026-27 Year 7/9 entry. Educators and school administrators can study this public-private partnership model as a template for curriculum integration and international collaboration. Students and families should evaluate both the international (IGCSE/A-Level) and local (HKDSE) pathway options based on university goals.

现就读Anchors小学部或子女年龄10-14岁的家长，应立即登记2026-27学年7年级或9年级的入学意向。教育工作者可参考此公私合作模式，作为课程融合与国际协作的范本。学生家庭则应依据升学目标，评估国际课程（IGCSE/A-Level）与本地文凭试（HKDSE）双轨路径的适合度。
