【是什么 / What it is】
香港海关捣毁一个涉嫌洗钱47亿港元（约6000万美元）的犯罪集团，该集团通过两家空壳公司在四年内进行超过2100笔交易，并同时经营毒品生意。调查显示，一名66岁男子作为这两家公司的唯一董事和股东，在六家银行开设了38个账户，声称从事电子产品进出口业务。

Hong Kong customs dismantled a criminal syndicate accused of laundering HK$4.7 billion (US$600 million) over four years through two shell companies that processed over 2,100 transactions while simultaneously running a drug operation. A 66-year-old man, serving as the sole director and shareholder of both firms since 2021, allegedly opened 38 accounts across six banks under the guise of electronics import-export business.

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】
此案揭示了利用空壳公司、频繁转账和多银行账户进行大规模洗钱的典型手法，其中120家关联公司大多无实际业务运营，单笔最大转账超3100万港元。这反映了跨境金融犯罪与毒品贸易的紧密关联，也凸显了监管机构对复杂洗钱网络的打击力度正在加强。

This case exposes classic money-laundering tactics involving shell companies, frequent transfers, and multiple bank accounts— with 120 linked firms having no real operations and the largest single transfer exceeding HK$31 million. It highlights the nexus between transnational financial crime and drug trafficking, while underscoring intensified regulatory crackdowns on sophisticated laundering networks.

【我能用什么 / How I can use it】
金融机构和合规从业者可将此案例用于反洗钱培训，重点识别"无实际业务的空壳公司+高频小额/大额混合转账+多账户分散操作"的风险模式。企业应加强客户尽职调查，对声称从事电子产品等易伪装行业但交易模式异常的客户提高警觉，并关注贸易背景真实性核查。

Financial institutions and compliance professionals can leverage this case for AML training, focusing on risk patterns of "shell companies with no real business + mixed high-frequency small and large transfers + multi-account dispersion operations." Enterprises should enhance customer due diligence, heighten scrutiny of clients claiming to operate in easily disguised sectors like electronics with abnormal transaction patterns, and prioritize verification of trade background authenticity.
