【是什么 / What it is】
香港古物咨询委员会批准了沙头角边境检查站重建项目的文物影响评估报告。该项目将拆除现有设施并新建两座以天桥连接的大楼，但附近具有法定古迹地位的协天宫（Hip Tin Temple）可能受到施工影响，引发关注。

Hong Kong's Antiquities Advisory Board approved the heritage impact assessment for the redevelopment of Sha Tau Kok border checkpoint. The project involves demolishing existing facilities and constructing two major buildings connected by a footbridge, though concerns were raised about potential impacts on nearby Hip Tin Temple, a declared monument.

This case exemplifies the typical tension between urban development and heritage conservation—the conflict between infrastructure modernization and historical preservation. Hip Tin Temple is not only a Kwan Tai temple built in 1894-1895, but also one of the few remaining main temples associated with the Tung Wo Market during the "Shap Yeuk" village alliance's economic dominance (1830s-1930s), carrying significant historical value.
