【是什么 / What it is】

埃塞俄比亚正申请将其拥有千年历史的咖啡仪式列入联合国教科文组织非物质文化遗产名录。这一传统仪式可追溯至9世纪，由女性主持，包含烘焙、研磨咖啡豆，并使用传统陶壶（jebena）分三次冲泡咖啡的完整流程。该申请由Warka咖啡公司推动，作为2026年埃塞俄比亚咖啡文化日的核心议题。

Ethiopia is seeking UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage status for its centuries-old coffee ceremony, which dates back to the 9th century. Traditionally performed by women, the ritual involves roasting and grinding coffee beans before brewing them three times in a jebena (traditional clay pot). The bid is being spearheaded by Warka Coffee as part of Ethiopian Coffee Culture Day 2026 in Addis Ababa.

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】

若申请成功，埃塞俄比亚咖啡仪式将成为继土耳其咖啡之后第二个获此殊荣的咖啡文化，有助于将咖啡从商品重新定义为文化载体。这不仅巩固了埃塞俄比亚作为全球咖啡起源地的历史地位，也为保护传统仪式、促进文化多样性提供了国际认可框架。相比之下，意大利浓缩咖啡的申遗尝试未能正式推进，凸显了该认定的竞争性与稀缺性。

If successful, it would become the second coffee-related heritage after Turkish coffee to receive this designation, reframing coffee from commodity to cultural vehicle. This would reinforce Ethiopia's historical position as coffee's birthplace while providing international recognition for preserving traditional rituals and cultural diversity. Notably, Italy's attempt to have espresso recognized was never formalized, highlighting the competitive and selective nature of this UNESCO designation.
