【是什么 / What it is】
新加坡国家发展部长徐芳达近日在国会宣布，将在牛车水珍珠山城市公园附近建造一座超过60层的超高层组屋项目，包含约1700个单位，这是该国公共住房"建屋局预购组屋"(BTO)计划中的新尝试，旨在通过集约化土地利用来增加住房供应。
Singapore's Minister for National Development Chee Hong Tat announced in parliament a new public housing project exceeding 60 storeys near Pearl's Hill City Park in Chinatown, comprising about 1,700 units. This represents a new attempt under the Build-To-Order (BTO) scheme to increase housing supply through intensified land use.

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】
这一举措标志着新加坡公共住房从近年常见的40层向超高层发展，反映了土地资源稀缺压力下城市垂直化建设的趋势；但分析师指出，由于建造成本高、场地限制多，以及政府为防止市中心房产投机而实施的更严格监管，这类超高层开发不太可能成为常态。
This signals a shift from the typical 40-storey BTO flats toward super-tall developments, reflecting the trend of vertical urban expansion under land scarcity pressures. However, analysts note such projects are unlikely to become the norm due to high construction costs, site constraints, and stricter regulations to prevent speculative investment in centrally located flats.
