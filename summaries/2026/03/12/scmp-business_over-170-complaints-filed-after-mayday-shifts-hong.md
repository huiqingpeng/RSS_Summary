【是什么 / What it is】
台湾摇滚乐队五月天取消原定于香港启德体育园的一场演唱会并改期至3月24日，引发大量歌迷不满。截至周四下午5点，香港消费者委员会已收到至少174宗投诉，涉及金额约48.5万港元，其中80宗来自本地居民，94宗来自境外（主要是中国大陆）。
Taiwanese rock band Mayday cancelled one of its scheduled concerts at Hong Kong's Kai Tak Sports Park and rescheduled it for March 24, sparking widespread fan dissatisfaction. As of 5pm Thursday, Hong Kong's Consumer Council received at least 174 complaints involving approximately HK$485,333, with 80 from local residents and 94 from outside Hong Kong, primarily mainland China.

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】
此事件凸显了大型跨境娱乐活动中的消费者权益保护难题，尤其是涉及退款机制、隐私保护和跨境沟通效率等核心议题。主办方提出的"免费彩排"补偿方案未能平息争议，反映出替代性解决方案与消费者期望之间存在明显落差，也为未来同类活动的危机管理提供了重要参考案例。
This incident highlights consumer protection challenges in large-scale cross-border entertainment events, particularly regarding refund mechanisms, privacy protection, and cross-border communication efficiency. The organiser's compensation offer of a "free rehearsal" failed to resolve the dispute, revealing a significant gap between alternative solutions and consumer expectations, while providing an important reference case for crisis management in future similar events.

When purchasing tickets for cross-border events, prioritize official platforms with clear refund terms and accessible customer service channels, and retain complete transaction records. When disputes arise, file complaints with local consumer protection agencies to apply pressure, while monitoring key information such as refund channels, timelines, and privacy protection measures announced by organisers, and consider collective action with other affected consumers if necessary.
