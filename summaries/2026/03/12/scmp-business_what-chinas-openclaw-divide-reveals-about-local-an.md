This article discusses the priority gap between China's local and central governments regarding the OpenClaw AI boom—local authorities rushed to promote AI adoption through subsidies and policies to boost economic growth, while Beijing swiftly intervened with security warnings emphasizing cybersecurity risks.

This divide reveals a recurring tension in China's governance system: the conflict between local governments' pursuit of short-term economic performance and the central government's emphasis on long-term security and stability. As AI technology rapidly evolves, this "development-first" versus "security-first" contradiction will repeatedly emerge across more tech sectors, influencing policy directions and business decisions.
