【是什么 / What it is】

一项发表于《Journal of Archaeological Science》的2026年新研究，分析了中国新石器时代（距今6000多年前）16个围产期婴儿（34-40孕周）的瓮棺葬遗址。研究发现古代中国人将死产、流产或出生后不久夭折的婴儿与较大婴儿分开安葬，置于特定区域的独立陶罐中，显示出对围产期死亡的特殊对待。

A 2026 study published in the *Journal of Archaeological Science* analyzed 16 urn burials of perinatal infants (34-40 gestational weeks) from over 6,000 years ago in Neolithic China. The research found that ancient Chinese people separated stillbirths, miscarriages, and newborns who died shortly after birth from older infants, placing them in separate jars within designated areas, indicating special treatment for perinatal deaths.

This discovery reveals that early Chinese societies held stage-based perceptions of "personhood"—perinatal infants were assigned distinct social identities and funerary rituals, reflecting ancient residents' unique concepts of life and death.
