【是什么 / What it is】
这是一起发生在香港的电信诈骗案件，诈骗者冒充立法会议员Gary Chan的身份，通过电话联系一名女性受害者，以虚假借口诱骗其转账38,000港元。该案件已被警方列为"以欺骗手段取得财产"案件，由新界南总区反诈骗小组调查。

This is a telecom fraud case in Hong Kong where scammers impersonated legislator Gary Chan, contacted a female victim by phone, and tricked her into transferring HK$38,000 under false pretenses. The case has been classified as "obtaining property by deception" and is being investigated by the Anti-Deception Team at the New Territories South regional headquarters.

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】
此案揭示了诈骗手段的新趋势——冒充公众人物尤其是受信任的政府官员，利用民众对公权力的信任实施犯罪，不仅造成直接经济损失，更严重损害社会信任基础。随着AI语音合成和深度伪造技术的发展，此类冒充诈骗可能变得更加难以识别，对公共安全构成持续威胁。

This case reveals an emerging trend in scam tactics—impersonating public figures, especially trusted government officials, to exploit public confidence in authority for criminal purposes, causing not only direct financial losses but also severely undermining social trust. With advancing AI voice synthesis and deepfake technologies, such impersonation scams may become increasingly difficult to detect, posing an ongoing threat to public safety.

Individuals should adopt a "verify first" security habit: for any call requesting money transfer, independently verify the caller's identity through official channels, never relying solely on caller ID or voice; stay updated on fraud alerts from police and banks to understand new tactics such as impersonating officials and AI voice cloning; if encountering suspicious situations, immediately contact anti-fraud hotlines or report to police, and preserve call records and transfer receipts as evidence.
