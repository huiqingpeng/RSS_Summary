【是什么 / What it is】
香港教育局局长蔡若莲表示，由于学龄人口结构性下降，15所公立小学因收生不足未能获批开办资助小一班级，这些学校需合并或自费运营，甚至可能面临停办。尽管当局已用尽"软着陆"措施，但收生不足问题仍未解决，她认为短期内关闭 struggling schools 可避免家长日后面临更大问题。

Hong Kong's Education Secretary Christine Choi stated that 15 public primary schools failed to secure approval for government-subsidised Primary One classes due to insufficient enrolment amid structural population decline. Despite exhausting "soft-landing" measures, under-enrolment persists, and she argued that closing struggling schools in the short term would prevent future problems for parents.

This reflects the profound impact of Hong Kong's demographic shift on its education system, where school surplus and shrinking pupil numbers have become unavoidable structural challenges. The policy pivot from "protecting schools" to "sustainable education development" signals a major adjustment period, with more mergers and closures likely ahead.
