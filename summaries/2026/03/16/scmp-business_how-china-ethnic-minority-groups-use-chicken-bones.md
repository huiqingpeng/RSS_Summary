This article introduces "Dajibu" (chicken divination), a centuries-old ritual practiced by ethnic minority groups in southwestern China where a rooster is beaten to death and its femur bones are interpreted to predict fortune. Originating from Tang and Song dynasties' spiritual beliefs tied to drum-beating for healing when medicine was unavailable, the custom remains prevalent today among groups including the Yi, Mosuo, Zhuang, and Yao.
