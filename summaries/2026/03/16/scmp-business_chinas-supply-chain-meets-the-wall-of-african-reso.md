【是什么 / What it is】
这篇文章讨论非洲资源富国（以津巴布韦为例）正通过出口禁令等政策加强对关键矿产的控制，导致全球价格上涨并冲击中国供应链。津巴布韦将原定于2027年的锂矿出口禁令提前实施，以迫使更多本地增值加工，而中国电池生产商约15%的锂精矿供应依赖该国，因此受到严重打击。
This article discusses how resource-rich African nations (with Zimbabwe as an example) are tightening control over critical minerals through policies like export bans, driving up global prices and disrupting Chinese supply chains. Zimbabwe moved up its lithium export ban from 2027 to force more local value-added processing, hitting Chinese battery producers hard as they rely on the country for about 15% of their lithium concentrate supply.

【为什么重要 / Why it matters】
这标志着"资源民族主义"在非洲的崛起，资源国不再满足于原材料出口，而是要求产业链本地化以获取更高附加值。对中国而言，这暴露了过度依赖单一来源的供应链脆弱性，同时也预示着全球关键矿产竞争格局正在重塑，投资不再能自动换取资源准入。
This signals the rise of "resource nationalism" in Africa, where resource-rich countries no longer settle for raw material exports but demand localized industrial chains for higher value capture. For China, it exposes supply chain vulnerabilities from over-reliance on single sources, while foreshadowing a reshaping of global critical minerals competition where investment no longer automatically guarantees resource access.

【我能用什么 / How I can use it】
企业应加速供应链多元化布局，投资非洲本地加工产能以符合政策导向，同时建立更灵活的资源采购网络。投资者需重新评估资源类海外项目的政治风险溢价，关注"资源民族主义"趋势下的新合作模式（如技术换资源、合资本地加工等）。
Companies should accelerate supply chain diversification, invest in local African processing capacity to align with policy trends, and build more flexible resource procurement networks. Investors need to reassess political risk premiums for overseas resource projects and explore new cooperation models under the "resource nationalism" trend, such as technology-for-resources exchanges or joint ventures for local processing.
