This article analyzes year-over-year changes in American consumers' beverage consumption at coffee shops and restaurants, based on transaction data from Toast's POS system covering over 160,000 food and beverage businesses, revealing declining sales of traditional teas and drip coffee alongside growth in espresso-based drinks and functional beverages.

This trend reflects a fundamental shift in consumer behavior: the convenience of home preparation is eroding retail market share for basic beverage categories, while espresso-based drinks requiring professional equipment maintain retail dominance; meanwhile, the rise of functional beverages signals consumers' growing prioritization of "efficacy" over mere caffeine intake.
