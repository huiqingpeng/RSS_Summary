This is a report on the first full day of zero commercial ship transits through the Strait of Hormuz due to US-Israel military strikes on Iran. Since the conflict began on February 28, the world's most critical oil transit chokepoint dropped to zero crossings on Saturday, with about 400 vessels clustering in the Gulf of Oman awaiting reopening while global shipping routes undergo major rerouting.
