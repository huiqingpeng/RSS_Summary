Iran war: Trump warns against mining Strait of Hormuz, Hegseth vows ‘intense’ US attacks
Defence Secretary Pete Hegseth pledged ‘most intense day of strikes’ yet, as tensions over critical Strait of Hormuz shipping lane escalate
Trump added that the US is using the same technology and missile capabilities deployed against drug traffickers to “permanently eliminate” any boat or ship attempting to mine the strait.
The US president’s latest threats against Iran came after media reports on Tuesday saying that Iran had begun laying mines in the strait to disrupt shipping in the key waterway.
Trump also said on social media minutes later that he was “pleased to report” that within the last few hours, the US had “hit, and completely destroyed, 10 inactive mine laying boats and/or ships, with more to follow”.
Tuesday will be the “most intense day of strikes” inside Iran so far, Hegseth said earlier in the day, adding that Trump will ultimately decide the war’s timeline.
