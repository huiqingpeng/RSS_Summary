Strait of Hormuz must stay blocked, Iran’s Mojtaba Khamenei says in defiant statement
In first declaration since his appointment as the country’s new supreme leader, Khamenei said all US military bases in region must close

Iran’s new supreme leader has vowed to keep blocking the Strait of Hormuz, attack US bases and avenge the blood of its martyrs in his first statement since succeeding his father.
“The lever of blocking the Strait of Hormuz must definitely be used,” Mojtaba Khamenei said about the strategic waterway, which carries about a fifth of global oil shipments.
Khamenei urged Iran’s Gulf neighbours to close US military bases in the region “as soon as possible, because they must have realised by now that the claim of establishing security and peace by America was nothing more than a lie”.

Also on Thursday, Iran’s central military command threatened to retaliate against the region’s oil and gas industry if its own infrastructure were attacked during the US-Israel military campaign.
“We will set the region’s oil and gas on fire with the slightest attack on Iran’s energy infrastructure and ports,” a spokesperson said.
Iran’s new leader was chosen in March by the Assembly of Experts, a group of senior clerics responsible for selecting the country’s supreme leader, after his father, Ali Khamenei, was killed in an air strike.
