Which new technologies are we seeing in coffee shops?
Key takeaways
- Coffee shops increasingly rely on tech stacks to manage rising operational complexity.
- Data replaces intuition when making critical decisions about café profitability.
- Point-of-sale systems now function as central command centres for operations.
- Advanced tools allow baristas to focus on customer interactions.
Coffee shops operate in a fundamentally different landscape than they did just five years ago. Customer expectations have evolved beyond coffee quality to encompass speed, convenience, and personalised experiences.
Meanwhile, café owners face mounting pressures: tighter profit margins, increased operational complexity, and competition from both independents and chains. In response, technology has shifted from a back-office afterthought to a strategic necessity.
Point-of-sale systems, in particular, have emerged as central command centres that shape how cafés understand their business, serve their customers, and plan for growth.
Corin Camenisch from SumUp and Bruno Damiano Maciel from Jungle Berry explain more.

Technology is playing a bigger role in coffee shops
Running a café today demands juggling far more variables than in the past. Menus have expanded to include plant milks, seasonal and signature drinks, and food offerings tailored to different dayparts.
Sales channels have also multiplied beyond traditional counter service to include takeaway orders, third-party delivery platforms, and subscription models. At the same time, operating costs continue to rise while profit margins remain stubbornly thin.
“Coffee shop owners are expected to do more with less,” says Corin Camenisch, the Global Marketing & Brand Strategy Lead at SumUp, a global financial technology company. “While economic insecurity continues to rise, so does customer expectation. Getting a coffee has become more than just transactional. It’s a social touchpoint, often a small but meaningful moment of reset in someone’s day.”
This complexity means traditional technology systems are insufficient for coffee shops. Point-of-sale platforms have evolved from simple cash registers into integrated ecosystems that connect payments, inventory management, sales analytics, and customer data. Cloud-based platforms enable real-time visibility across locations and devices, while data-driven insights replace intuition-based decision-making.
“Modern POS systems have become crucial to café operations, linking front-of-house service, back-office inventory, and the customer experience,” Corin notes.
All-in-one devices like SumUp Terminal consolidate operational tools into a single touchpoint, while POS platforms like SumUp POS provide actionable insights across sales, stock, and staffing. These systems are increasingly shaping how cafés understand their own performance and potential.
Why technology is becoming a long-term strategic decision
Alongside investments in automated pour-over brewers and advanced espresso machines, the technology stack that supports business operations has become equally critical. Café owners increasingly rely on data rather than intuition to make decisions that affect profitability and growth.
Bruno, the founder and CEO of Jungle Berry, experienced this transition firsthand. Before adopting SumUp, his café managed payments, inventory, and operations through fragmented systems that required extensive manual input. “This made it challenging to get a clear, real-time picture of performance, and staff’s attention was spent on operational distractions rather than delivering attentive and personalised customer service,” he says.
The motivation to upgrade stemmed from efficiency and scalability needs. “We wanted one platform that could handle ordering, payments, and insights in a simple, intuitive way, without slowing service or making things harder for staff,” he explains. “The industry is fast-paced, running on tight margins, especially during peak hours, so even small delays cause considerable disruption.”
Tools like SumUp Terminal and POS address these pressures by allowing cafés to oversee all operations from a unified platform, improve efficiency through streamlined workflows, reduce costs via better inventory and staffing insights, and maintain the flexibility to adapt as business needs evolve.
The data generated by these systems also reveals patterns that challenge longstanding assumptions. “Trading patterns, for example, are often more nuanced than owners expect,” Corin explains. “Data may show that weekend brunch traffic starts earlier than anticipated, or that weekday afternoons remain steadily busy rather than slowing down.”
Bruno agrees, saying: “We were surprised by how much customer behaviour varied throughout the day. Certain items sold far better during specific time windows than we expected, which helped us adjust promotions and menu visibility accordingly.”
The next phase of coffee shop technology
As automation continues to advance in coffee shops – whether supporting training, freeing baristas to focus on customer interaction, or improving extraction consistency – the question becomes: where will technology evolve next?
Potential developments include predictive analytics and AI-driven demand forecasting to anticipate inventory needs and staffing requirements, deeper integration between POS systems, loyalty programmes, and customer data to create more personalised experiences, and modular technology ecosystems that enable cafés to customise their setups to specific operational needs.
“Cafés that prioritise adaptability, smart use of technology, and a focus on the customer experience will have an unrivalled advantage, and tools like SumUp‘s card readers and POS will play a pivotal role in transformation,” Corin says.
Looking ahead, as customers seek more intentional hospitality experiences, it’s essential that technology enhances rather than replaces the human element of coffee shops.
“The right tools help cafés run more efficiently, manage costs, and deliver a smoother customer experience,” Bruno explains. “But business owners must not see this as an opportunity to take the back seat, rather as a chance to preserve the human side of hospitality that prioritises meaningful connection.”
The transformation of coffee shop technology from a peripheral tool to a strategic asset reflects broader shifts in consumer expectations and operational realities. As cafés navigate rising costs, expanding service channels, and increasingly sophisticated customer demands, integrated POS platforms have become essential infrastructure.
The data they generate informs everything from menu design to staffing schedules, while their flexibility supports new business models and revenue streams.
