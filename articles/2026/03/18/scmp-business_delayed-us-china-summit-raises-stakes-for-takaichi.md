Delayed US-China summit raises stakes for Takaichi ahead of White House visit
Analysts said combination of Iran war and new date for Trump’s visit to Beijing could put Japan’s leader on the defensive.
Takaichi will be trying to finesse shifting tariff levels, huge US investment demands and criticism over Tokyo’s hesitation at deploying support ships to the Strait of Hormuz, they said.
“Back then, her primary concern was getting into Trump’s ear before he headed off to his summit with Xi in Beijing. You had really positive momentum,” said Jeremy Chan, senior analyst with the Eurasia Group.
“And now the double whammy of the Iran war and the delay to the China trip. It’s higher stakes, but for the wrong reason.”
Analysts said Takaichi could find Trump keen to have a “normal” summit that distracts from the Middle East – or he could light into her for failing to support the unpopular war and lash out, rhetorically and economically.
