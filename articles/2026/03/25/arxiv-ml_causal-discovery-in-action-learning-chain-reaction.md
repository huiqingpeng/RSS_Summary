Title:Causal Discovery in Action: Learning Chain-Reaction Mechanisms from Interventions
View PDF HTML (experimental)Abstract:Causal discovery is challenging in general dynamical systems because, without strong structural assumptions, the underlying causal graph may not be identifiable even from interventional data. However, many real-world systems exhibit directional, cascade-like structure, in which components activate sequentially and upstream failures suppress downstream effects. We study causal discovery in such chain-reaction systems and show that the causal structure is uniquely identifiable from blocking interventions that prevent individual components from activating. We propose a minimal estimator with finite-sample guarantees, achieving exponential error decay and logarithmic sample complexity. Experiments on synthetic models and diverse chain-reaction environments demonstrate reliable recovery from a few interventions, while observational heuristics fail in regimes with delayed or overlapping causal effects.
