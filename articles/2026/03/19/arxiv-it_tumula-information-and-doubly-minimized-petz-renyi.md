Title:Tumula information and doubly minimized Petz Renyi lautum information
View PDFAbstract:We study a doubly minimized variant of the lautum information - a reversed analogue of the mutual information - defined as the minimum relative entropy between any product state and a fixed bipartite quantum state; we refer to this measure as the tumula information. In addition, we introduce the corresponding Petz Renyi version, which we call the doubly minimized Petz Renyi lautum information (PRLI). We derive several general properties of these correlation measures and provide an operational interpretation in the context of hypothesis testing. Specifically, we show that the reverse direct exponent of certain binary quantum state discrimination problems is quantified by the doubly minimized PRLI of order $\alpha\in (0,1/2)$, and that the Sanov exponent is determined by the tumula information. Furthermore, we investigate the extension of the tumula information to channels and compare its properties with previous results on the channel umlaut information [Girardi et al., arXiv:2503.21479].
