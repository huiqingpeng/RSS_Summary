Laser Ranging Makes GPS Satellites More Accurate

是什么 / What it is

这篇文章介绍了在最新发射的GPS卫星上安装激光反射器阵列（LRAs）的技术创新，使地面激光能够精确测量与卫星之间的距离。这项技术类似于阿波罗任务留在月球上的反射器，主要用于大地测量学等科学研究领域。
This article introduces a technical innovation of installing Laser Retroreflector Arrays (LRAs) on newly launched GPS satellites, enabling ground-based lasers to accurately measure the distance to these satellites. Similar to the retroreflector array left on the Moon during the Apollo missions, this technology is primarily used for scientific pursuits such as geodesy.

为什么重要 / Why it matters

这项技术显著提升了GPS系统的测量精度，为研究地球形状、重力和自转的大地测量学提供了关键数据。同时，它还能精确校准卫星高度计，提高长期测量的准确性，对地球观测卫星和科学研究具有重要价值。
This technology significantly enhances the measurement accuracy of GPS systems, providing crucial data for geodesy—the study of Earth's shape, gravity, and rotation. It also enables precise calibration of satellite altimeters and improves the accuracy of long-term measurements, offering substantial value for Earth-observing satellites and scientific research.

我能用什么 / How I can use it

For professionals in GIS, surveying, or Earth sciences, you can leverage LRA-enhanced GPS data to improve project accuracy. Developers can explore integrating high-precision positioning data into autonomous driving, precision agriculture, or disaster monitoring systems, while tracking the deployment progress of GPS III satellites to plan technical upgrades.
