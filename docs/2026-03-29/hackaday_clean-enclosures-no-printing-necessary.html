This article introduces a method for creating professional-looking electronics enclosures without a 3D printer, using only hand tools. The author [mircemk] uses thin PVC sheeting (from sign shops, up to 5mm thick), bends and cuts it by hand, drills holes, bonds pieces with cyanoacrylate glue, and applies bold contrasting colors with peel-and-stick wallpaper.

This approach offers electronics hobbyists a low-barrier, high-quality alternative without the learning curve of 3D printing. Compared to 3D-printed enclosures, the hand-crafted PVC technique can sometimes achieve more professional-looking results, while using readily available materials, simple tools, and a mess-free process (no painting required).
