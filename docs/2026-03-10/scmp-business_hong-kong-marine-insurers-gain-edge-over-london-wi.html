Hong Kong marine insurers gain edge over London with cheaper war-risk cover

What it is
香港保险业监管局去年11月支持本地保险公司成立了特殊战争风险保险池，为航行于海湾地区的10艘中国大陆船舶提供战争和紧急风险保障，最高赔偿额达1.3亿美元。这是香港挑战伦敦在全球海上保险市场主导地位的最新举措，旨在利用地缘优势为船东提供更具成本效益的保险方案。
The Insurance Authority of Hong Kong supported local insurers in launching a special war-risk insurance pool in November, which now covers 10 mainland Chinese ships sailing in the Gulf with up to US$130 million in compensation against war and emergency risks. This represents Hong Kong's latest move to challenge London's dominance in global marine insurance by leveraging geographic advantages to offer more cost-effective coverage for shipowners.

Rising Middle East tensions have highlighted the strategic necessity of war-risk coverage, while Hong Kong's cost advantage significantly enhances its competitiveness as a regional marine insurance hub. This development reduces Chinese shipowners' reliance on London markets and reflects the long-term shift of global marine insurance from traditional Western centers toward Asia.
