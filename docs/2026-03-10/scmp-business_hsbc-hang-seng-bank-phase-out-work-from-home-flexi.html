HSBC, Hang Seng Bank phase out work-from-home flexibility for frontline staff in Hong Kong

SCMP Business 2026-03-10
Read Original Article →

是什么 / What it is

香港最大银行汇丰银行（HSBC）及其子公司恒生银行（Hang Seng Bank）宣布从4月1日起取消前线员工（包括交易员和销售人员）的居家办公弹性安排，要求客户-facing员工每周至少回办公室3-4天。这是香港金融业在后疫情时代收紧工作模式的最新举措。
HSBC, Hong Kong's largest bank, and its subsidiary Hang Seng Bank announced the elimination of work-from-home flexibility for frontline staff—including traders and salespeople—starting April 1, requiring client-facing employees to return to the office 3-4 days per week. This marks the latest move in Hong Kong's financial sector to tighten workplace policies in the post-pandemic era.

为什么重要 / Why it matters

这一政策反映了全球金融行业"重返办公室"（Return to Office, RTO）趋势的加速，尤其强调管理层以身作则的重要性；同时也显示香港作为国际金融中心，正通过强化实体办公来维护协作效率、合规监管和企业文化。对于从业者而言，这意味着工作-生活平衡的重大转变，也可能影响人才流动和雇主品牌竞争力。
This policy reflects the accelerating global financial industry trend of "Return to Office" (RTO), particularly emphasizing the importance of leadership by example; it also signals Hong Kong's efforts as an international financial center to reinforce in-person collaboration, compliance oversight, and corporate culture through physical presence. For professionals, this represents a significant shift in work-life balance and may impact talent mobility and employer brand competitiveness.
