This article introduces a novel high-current switch design. The creator [Technology Hobby] developed a V-shaped channel structure to address the problems of insufficient contact area and arcing in traditional switches under extreme current conditions (equivalent to about 400 car batteries), using a 3D-printed frame filled with concrete for rigidity.

High-current experiments are increasingly popular among makers and electronics hobbyists, yet switches often become the weak link in the system—poor contact leads to heat, sparks, and equipment damage. This design offers a more reliable solution for high-current applications by maximizing contact area and preventing contact bounce, while demonstrating that industrial-grade performance can be achieved with low-cost DIY methods (3D printing + concrete).
