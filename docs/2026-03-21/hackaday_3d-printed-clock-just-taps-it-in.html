是什么 / What it is

This article introduces a 3D-printed golf-themed clock designed by hobbyist Jason. Powered by an ESP32-C3 microcontroller, the clock uses two servos to drive a miniature golfer figure that putts a golf ball into a hole every hour as a time indicator, with nearly all components being 3D printed.

This project demonstrates how modern manufacturing technologies (3D printing, affordable microcontrollers, servos) dramatically lower the barrier for creative mechanical builds, enabling individuals to replicate and reimagine traditional complex mechanisms like cuckoo clocks. It also embodies the maker culture's design philosophy of "functional imitation + thematic innovation," offering inspiration for personalized home automation.
