China mall shuffle: as EVs exit, who’s filling the ground-floor showrooms?

SCMP Business 2026-03-15

是什么 / What it is

中国一线城市的核心商圈购物中心正经历租户结构转型：曾经占据黄金一层展厅的新能源汽车品牌因销量低迷纷纷撤店，取而代之的是潮玩、运动服饰和机器人公司等新兴品牌。这一"商场洗牌"现象反映了中国零售地产市场的动态调整与消费趋势的快速演变。
Prime ground-floor spaces in China's tier-one city malls are undergoing a tenant restructuring: EV brands that once dominated these premium showrooms are exiting due to sluggish sales, being replaced by emerging players in designer toys, athletic wear, and robotics. This "mall shuffle" reflects the dynamic adjustment of China's retail real estate market and the rapid evolution of consumer trends.

为什么重要 / Why it matters

这一转变标志着商业地产逻辑的根本变化——从追求品牌光环转向注重实际客流转化与租金收益，生活方式、餐饮和体验式零售正成为商场运营的核心竞争力。同时，它也揭示了消费市场的结构性变迁：情绪消费（潮玩）、健康生活方式（运动品牌）和前沿科技体验（机器人）正在取代大宗耐用品展示，成为吸引消费者的新引擎。
This shift marks a fundamental change in commercial real estate logic—from pursuing brand prestige to focusing on actual foot traffic conversion and rental income, with lifestyle, dining, and experiential retail becoming core competitive advantages. It also reveals structural changes in consumer markets: emotional consumption (designer toys), healthy lifestyles (athletic brands), and cutting-edge tech experiences (robotics) are replacing big-ticket durable goods displays as new engines for attracting consumers.

For retail practitioners or investors, focus on brand opportunities in the "experiential consumption" track, especially formats combining product display with interactive experiences; for commercial real estate operators, establish flexible tenant adjustment mechanisms prioritizing high sales-per-square-meter, high-repeat-visit tenant mixes; for consumer brands, learn from Unitree Robotics and Pop Mart's model by transforming offline spaces into brand experience and community operation hubs rather than pure sales channels.
