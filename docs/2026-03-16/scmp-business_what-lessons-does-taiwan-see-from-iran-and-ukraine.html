What lessons does Taiwan see from Iran and Ukraine for its air-defence strategy?

Read Original Article →

是什么 / What it is

台湾防务部门向立法机构提交报告，指出需要发展低成本拦截武器来应对中国大陆日益增长的远程火箭弹和无人机威胁。该报告借鉴了美伊以之间的导弹无人机攻防战以及俄乌战争的经验，认为传统昂贵的防空导弹系统难以应对大规模廉价武器的饱和攻击。
Taiwan's defence ministry submitted a report to lawmakers stating that low-cost interceptor weapons are urgently needed to counter the mainland Chinese military's growing arsenal of long-range rockets and drones. Drawing lessons from recent missile and drone exchanges between the US, Israel, and Iran, as well as the Ukraine war, the report argues that traditional expensive air-defence systems cannot withstand saturation attacks by large numbers of cheap weapons.

这一战略转变反映了现代战争形态的根本变化：低成本、大规模、智能化的武器系统正在颠覆传统的"以质取胜"防御理念。对台湾而言，这不仅是军事技术路线的调整，更涉及如何在有限预算下建立可持续的防御体系，同时也为其他面临不对称威胁的国家和地区提供了重要参考。
This strategic shift reflects a fundamental change in modern warfare: low-cost, large-scale, and intelligent weapon systems are disrupting the traditional "quality over quantity" defence philosophy. For Taiwan, this is not merely a military technical adjustment, but involves building a sustainable defence system within limited budgets, while also providing important reference for other countries and regions facing asymmetric threats.
