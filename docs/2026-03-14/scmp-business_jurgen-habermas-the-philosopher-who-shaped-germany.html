Jürgen Habermas was one of Germany's most influential contemporary philosophers, renowned for his "theory of communicative action" and rational consensus-building in the public sphere. He died on June 21, 2025, at age 96, leaving behind a seven-decade academic and public career that profoundly shaped post-war Germany's moral discourse and political self-reflection.
