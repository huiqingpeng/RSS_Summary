From studio to market: Hong Kong Design Institute’s transdisciplinary path to global design impact

是什么 / What it is

香港设计学院（HKDI）是一所成立于2007年的设计教育机构，通过融合"东西方交汇"的文化基因与跨学科设计科技教育，培养面向亚洲市场的创意人才。学院采用"思与做"（Think and Do）的教学理念，将概念严谨性与原型制作、商业营销思维相结合，并配备行业级设施与深度产业合作网络。
The Hong Kong Design Institute (HKDI) is a design education institution established in 2007 that cultivates creative talent for Asian markets by integrating Hong Kong's "East Meets West" cultural DNA with transdisciplinary design and technology education. Guided by its "Think and Do" pedagogy, the institute combines conceptual rigor with prototyping, testing, and business marketing thinking, supported by industry-grade facilities and deep industry partnerships. ---

为什么重要 / Why it matters

HKDI代表了职业教育（VPET）与创意产业融合的新范式，其毕业生不仅服务于本地文创产业，更在全国及区域层面推动增长。在全球创意经济竞争加剧的背景下，这种"从工作室到市场"的培养模式直接回应了产业对既具技术敏捷性又懂商业转化的复合型人才的迫切需求。
HKDI represents a new paradigm where vocational and professional education and training (VPET) converges with creative industries, with graduates driving growth not only locally but nationally and regionally. Amid intensifying global competition in the creative economy, this "studio to market" cultivation model directly addresses industry's urgent demand for versatile talent possessing both technical agility and commercialization capabilities. ---
