This article explores artistic expressions emerging from hacker/maker culture, particularly focusing on "circuit sculpture" and other forms that combine electronic engineering with aesthetic pursuits. Starting from projects in the Hackaday community, the author presents multiple cases where functional hardware is transformed into visual art, revealing the unique aesthetic perspective of the technical community.

这种"技术艺术"打破了工程与艺术的学科壁垒，证明实用性和美感可以共存于同一创作中，为跨领域创新提供了新范式。随着3D打印、开源硬件等工具普及，越来越多的创作者能够低成本地实现复杂机械美学，这种趋势正在重塑我们对"艺术家"和"工程师"身份边界的理解。
This "technical art" breaks down disciplinary barriers between engineering and art, demonstrating that functionality and beauty can coexist in a single creation, offering a new paradigm for cross-domain innovation. As tools like 3D printing and open-source hardware become more accessible, more creators can achieve complex mechanical aesthetics at low cost—reshaping our understanding of the boundaries between "artist" and "engineer" identities.
