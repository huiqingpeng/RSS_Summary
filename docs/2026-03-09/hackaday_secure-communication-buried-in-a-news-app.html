是什么 /

What it is
CoverDrop 是由《卫报》与剑桥大学开发者合作设计的一种隐蔽通信系统，将加密消息功能嵌入新闻应用程序中。该系统让用户能够在不暴露通信行为的前提下，向记者提交机密线索。它通过将真实消息与大量"掩护消息"混合，使监控者无法从网络流量或设备存储中察觉任何异常。
CoverDrop is a covert communication system developed by The Guardian and Cambridge University developers, embedding encrypted messaging functionality directly into a news app. It allows users to submit confidential tips to journalists without revealing that any communication is taking place. The system mixes real messages with numerous "cover messages" so that surveillance cannot detect anomalies in network traffic or device storage. ---

为什么重要 / Why it matters

在高压监控环境下，单纯使用加密通信应用本身就可能成为被针对的证据——"你有秘密"这一事实即构成风险。CoverDrop 解决了"元数据泄露"和"存在性暴露"问题，为举报人、记者和异见人士提供了更高层次的 plausible deniability（合理否认性）。这一模式可能推动新闻业安全基础设施的范式转变，从"加密内容"升级为"隐藏通信行为本身"。
In high-surveillance environments, merely using an encrypted messaging app can itself become evidence against you—the fact that you "have something to hide" becomes the risk. CoverDrop addresses metadata leakage and "existence exposure," providing whistleblowers, journalists, and dissidents with stronger plausible deniability. This model could drive a paradigm shift in journalistic security infrastructure, evolving from "encrypting content" to "hiding the very act of communication." ---
