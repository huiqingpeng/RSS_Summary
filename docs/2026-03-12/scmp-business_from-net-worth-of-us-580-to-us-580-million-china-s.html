This is an entrepreneurial story about Liu Huiping, a 49-year-old entrepreneur from Anhui province, China. A secondary school dropout who launched his first steamed bun shop in Shanghai in 1998 with a 4,000-yuan (US$580) loan, he built a bun empire generating 180 million yuan annually after surviving failure, violent attacks, and bankruptcy. ---

Liu's story exemplifies the "low-start, high-growth" model of China's private economy, demonstrating how traditional food industries can achieve scale through standardization and franchising. His unique wealth philosophy—renting instead of buying property and believing wealth should not be inherited—offers thought-provoking perspectives on entrepreneurial culture and intergenerational wealth transfer. ---
